We're About to Play Quidditch, Y'all

A Very Potter Primer of Ruby Instance Methods

February 13, 2015

You're Harry Potter! (Or, if you'd rather be Malfoy, go crazy.) And we're going to play some Quidditch, by way of ruby instance methods.

Why do we use ruby instance methods? Well, instance methods allow us to keep track of our variables across different methods. Let's take our game of Quidditch as an example. We're going to want to keep track of score, shoot, and chase the snitch, while keeping both teams constant. This means we'll be using a bunch of different methods but need to keep certain variables stable throughout each. And that's why we create a class.

Before we start creating our game, here's quick refresher of the rules of Quidditch:

  • If your team shoots the quaffle through one of the opponent's hoops, you get 10 points.
  • If your team catches the snitch, the game is over, and you get 150 points. If the 150 points knocks your score above the other team's score, you win! Otherwise, they win.
