Car Conflict

March 3, 2015

In my family, we always had car troubles. Namely, we had far more people than cars, and each person had a place to go.

Generally it went as follows: the grown-ups had to go to work, so got priority; the teens had to duke it out for a chance at the wheel.

Generally, the parents would be the final deciders of who was going to get the car for the evening. How they calculated their decisions could, at times, be a source of pain to me and my brother. One persons' needs, time, and choices could be considered less than the others', which never feels great.

There were probably times when I felt I was being ignored, that I could have talked to my parents and brother. I could have explained why certain events were important for me to attend. Just saying, "Hey, it's really important to me to spend time with these friends/go to xyz function because I've been feeling [feeling] and I think going would help me not feel [feeling]." This, of course, should come in tandem with the freedom for my brother to make similar requests.

Basically, if there is a conflict, sit with yourself and analyze your motives, and then feel comfortable explaining those motives to people.
